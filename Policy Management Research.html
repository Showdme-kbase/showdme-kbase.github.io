The Policy Blueprint for Compliance

To bridge the gap between regulations and operations, SNFs must implement a vast array of policies. Our platform must help them create, manage, and enforce these policies across four critical domains. Click each tab to explore the essential policies.

  • Resident Care Policies: Guiding all clinical protocols and professional standards.
  • Complaint Management: Procedures for receiving, investigating, and resolving complaints.
  • Safe Patient Handling (SPH): Mandating use of mechanical lifts to prevent injuries.
  • Fall Prevention: Multifaceted approach to reduce fall risks (lighting, assistive devices).
  • Abuse Prevention and Response: Clear protocols for preventing and reporting abuse.
  • Infection Prevention and Control: Rigorous hand hygiene and outbreak management strategies.
  • Billing Accuracy: Ensuring claims reflect services provided and use correct codes.
  • PDPM Coding & Documentation: Mandating audits to align clinical docs with billing.
  • Related-Party Transactions: Ensuring all deals are at fair market value to prevent "tunneling".
  • Overpayment Management: Procedures for identifying and promptly refunding overpayments.
  • Internal Audit Program: Routine audits of billing, ethics, and compliance practices.
  • Hazard Communication Program: Informing employees about chemical hazards (SDS).
  • Bloodborne Pathogens Plan: Protecting staff from exposure to HIV, Hepatitis B/C.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Policy: Mandating provision and use of appropriate PPE.
  • Respiratory Protection Program: Governing respirator use, including fit testing and training.
  • Workplace Violence Prevention: Proactive strategies to ensure a safe environment.
  • HIPAA Privacy Rule Policies: Governing permissible uses and disclosures of PHI.
  • HIPAA Security Rule Policies: Safeguards for electronic PHI (access control, encryption).
  • Breach Notification Policies: Detailed steps for identifying and responding to data breaches.
  • Risk Assessment Policies: Mandating regular, comprehensive HIPAA risk analyses.
  • Employee Training Policies: Mandating and documenting regular HIPAA training for all staff.
